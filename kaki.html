Big Bat Kakiage & Mandrake Tempura

Ingredients:

  • 1 Onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 Large Carrot, peeled and cut into thin strips
  • 1/4 lb Scallops, thinly sliced
  • 1 Large Egg
  • 1 Cup Ice Water
  • 1 Cup All-purpose Flour
  • Vegetable Oil, for frying

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Gather the onion, carrot, scallops, and other ingredients.
  2. Make the Batter: Beat the egg in a large bowl. Add the ice water and sifted flour. Mix lightly to combine.
  3. Heat the Oil: Heat about four inches of vegetable oil to 340°F in a deep pan over medium to medium-high heat.
  4. Mix the Vegetables and Seafood: Add the onion slices, carrot strips, and scallops to the batter, mixing them in gently.
  5. Fry the Tempura: Scoop the batter mixture with a spoon and drop it into the hot oil. Use chopsticks or tongs to shape the pieces while frying.
  6. Cook Until Golden: Deep-fry the tempura until it is golden brown on both sides.
  7. Drain and Serve: Drain the tempura on a cooling rack to remove excess oil. Serve with tempura dipping sauce.

Tips:

  • Always mix the batter with chopsticks or a spoon to avoid overmixing. Overmixing makes the batter heavy.
  • Use icy-cold water for the batter to make it stick together and absorb less oil.
  • Ensure the oil is hot before frying. Test it by dropping a bit of batter into the oil—if it floats and sizzles, it's ready.
  • Cut the vegetables and seafood to similar sizes for consistent texture and even cooking.
  • Smaller pieces will result in a better crunch and fully cooked interior.
  • After frying, drain the tempura on a cooling rack to avoid excess oil. Tempura is best when eaten fresh.
  • If you have leftovers, reheat by briefly dipping them in hot oil to restore the crispiness.
